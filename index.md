## About

A series of hands-on computational workshops covering core concepts needed for
computational literacy and performing reproducible research in bioinformatics
and computational biology. 

## Workshops

All workshops will approach the use of LLMs and agentic coding with a principled
framework drawn from standard software engineering practices including specification
documents, and test-driven development. Examples will begin with a toy or artificial
example before introducing a relevant biological use case. 

| # | Week | Topic | Description |
|---|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Week 1 | Large Language Models | Conceptual overview of LLMs and responsible use |
| 2 | Week 2 | Linux/Bash and SCC | Command line fundamentals and cluster computing |
| 3 | Week 3 | Version Control | Git and collaborative development workflows |
| 4 | Week 4 | R | Data analysis and statistical computing in R |
| 5 | Week 5 | Python | Scientific computing and scripting in Python |
| 6 | Week 6 | FAIR Principles | Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable data practices |
| 7 | Week 7 | Machine Learning | Core ML concepts and applications |

| 9 | Week 9 | Data Visualization | Principles and tools for scientific visualization |

# AI Usage in these workshops

These workshops use the AI Assessment Scale (AIAS) (Perkins, Furze, Roe and MacVaugh, 2024)
to make AI policy transparent and consistent across all activities. The AIAS
describes five levels of AI involvement in assessment, from no AI to full
AI-human collaboration. Rather than a single blanket policy, each
component of these workshops has an assigned level that reflects its
learning goals. The level tells you both what level of use is expected
and why that choice was made.

The goal is to use AI in ways that serve your learning rather than
substitute for it.

## AIAS Levels at a Glance

| Level | Name | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **No AI** | Completed entirely without AI assistance |
| 2 | **AI Planning** | AI may support brainstorming and structuring; not present in final submission |
| 3 | **AI Collaboration** | AI assists in developing and refining work; human judgment directs and evaluates output |
| 4 | **Full AI** | AI used throughout; all AI-generated content must be cited and critically evaluated |
| 5 | **AI Exploration** | Open-ended co-design with AI; boundaries defined collaboratively |
